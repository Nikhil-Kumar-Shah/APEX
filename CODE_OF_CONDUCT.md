# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of the APEX project pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and technically excellent community.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive behaviour that strengthens our community

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness in all interactions.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experience levels.
- Giving and gracefully receiving constructive feedback on code, documentation, and ideas.
- Accepting responsibility, apologising when we make mistakes, and learning from them.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and the community, not just individual preferences.
- Helping newcomers get oriented — everyone was a beginner once.
- Writing clear, detailed issue reports and pull requests that respect reviewers' time.

### Unacceptable behaviour

- Sexualised language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention in any form.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment of any community member.
- Publishing others' private information (email, address, etc.) without explicit permission.
- Deliberately submitting malicious code, spam issues, or low-effort PRs designed to waste maintainer time.
- Persistent unsolicited contact after being asked to stop.
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a professional setting.

---

## APEX-Specific Community Standards

Because APEX is a technical infrastructure project, the following additional standards apply:

- **Accuracy matters**: Do not make claims about features, capabilities, or benchmarks that you cannot substantiate. This project is used by developers who depend on accurate documentation.
- **Respect the maintainers' time**: Issues, PRs, and discussions should be detailed and well-prepared before submission. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).
- **Security disclosures**: Security vulnerabilities must always be reported privately. Never disclose a vulnerability publicly before the maintainer has had the opportunity to address it. See [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They will take appropriate, fair, and proportionate corrective action in response to behaviour they determine to be inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right to:
- Edit, remove, or reject comments, commits, issues, PRs, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.
- Temporarily or permanently ban contributors whose behaviour is harmful to the community.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all official APEX community spaces, including:
- The GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews, wiki)
- GitHub Discussions
- Any official communication channels established by the maintainers

It also applies when an individual is officially representing APEX in public spaces (e.g., conferences, social media posts in an official capacity).

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project maintainers at:

**[https://www.nikhilkshah.me/contact](https://www.nikhilkshah.me/contact)**

All reports will be:
- Reviewed promptly and treated as confidential.
- Investigated fairly, with the reporter's privacy protected.
- Addressed with a proportionate response based on the severity and context.

Maintainers who do not uphold the Code of Conduct may face consequences as determined by other project leaders.
